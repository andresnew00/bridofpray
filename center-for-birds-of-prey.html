The Problem

Every year, thousands of raptors in South Carolina are hit by cars, tangled in power lines, poisoned, or shot.

🦅

They can't wait for help

An eagle with a broken wing or a hawk tangled in fence wire needs expert care within hours. Without trained responders and a real medical clinic, they don't survive.

🌍

Birds signal what's wrong with our world

Raptors sit at the top of the food chain. When they decline, it means something in our ecosystem is breaking. Lead poisoning, habitat loss, and chemical exposure show up in birds before they show up in us.

📚

The next generation won't protect what they've never seen

Thousands of South Carolina students have never stood 10 feet from a live Bald Eagle or watched a Peregrine Falcon stoop from the sky. Without that experience, conservation is just a word in a textbook.

Why the Center

35 Years of Proven, Professional Bird Care

Founded in 1991 as the Charleston Raptor Center, the Avian Conservation Center is the only nonprofit of its kind in South Carolina. Our medical clinic runs 365 days a year. Our educational programs reach thousands of students annually. And our oil spill facility is the only permanent one of its kind on the entire Eastern Seaboard.

We don't just treat injured birds. We research environmental threats, protect endangered species like the Swallow-tailed Kite, and give the public a living connection to the wild world that is vanishing around them.

Common Questions

Everything you might be wondering

How does my monthly gift get used?
Your donation directly funds the daily operations of our Avian Medical Clinic, educational programs for students and adults, conservation research, and our emergency oil spill response team. We run on contributions — your gift keeps the lights on, the clinic stocked, and the birds healing.
Can I cancel my monthly gift if I need to?
Absolutely. You can pause or cancel anytime, no questions asked. Just email us at info@thecenterforbirdsofprey.org or call 843-971-7474 and we'll take care of it immediately. We'd rather have you give what you can than feel locked in.
Is my donation tax-deductible?
Yes. The Avian Conservation Center is a 501(c)3 nonprofit organization. All contributions are tax-deductible as provided by law. You'll receive a donation receipt for your records after each gift.
How do I know my money is really helping birds?
We treat 800 to 1,000 birds every year and have cared for more than 16,000 since 1991. We send monthly donors real impact updates, including stories about specific birds currently in our care. You'll know exactly what your gift is doing.
What kinds of birds does the Center treat?
We specialize in birds of prey and shorebirds including Bald Eagles, Red-tailed Hawks, Great Horned Owls, Peregrine Falcons, Osprey, Swallow-tailed Kites, and more. Please note: we are not able to treat ducks, geese, or songbirds.
What is the SC Oiled Bird Treatment Facility?
It's a 7,000 sq ft facility built with a $1.8 million federal grant, designated by the U.S. Coast Guard as South Carolina's official response center for oil-contaminated birds. It is the only permanent facility of its kind on the Eastern Seaboard.
Can I give a gift in someone's memory or honor?
Yes, and it's a beautiful way to celebrate someone special. Tribute gifts are a meaningful option. Contact us at 843-971-7474 or info@thecenterforbirdsofprey.org and we will send an acknowledgement letter to the person you wish to honor.
Does my company match donations?
Many do! Ask your HR department or check your company's giving portal. Matching gifts can double or even triple your impact. We're happy to assist with any paperwork — just reach out to us directly.
What does the Wing Guard membership include?
Wing Guard members receive monthly impact email updates, behind-the-scenes stories from the clinic, early access to events, and the deep satisfaction of knowing a real bird is alive because of you. Higher tiers include recognition on our donor wall and exclusive content.
I found an injured bird. What do I do?
Call us immediately at 843-971-7474 and press option 1 for the Injured Bird Line. We answer from 9am to 9pm weekdays and 9am to 5pm weekends. Do not offer the bird food or water, and keep it in a warm, dark, quiet container until transport can be arranged.
